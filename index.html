About Me

"I’m LeBron James — a kid from Akron, Ohio, who dreamed big and put in the work to make it real. I entered the NBA in 2003 as the first overall pick by the Cleveland Cavaliers. As an 18-year-old, I knew I had a lot to prove — not just to the league, but to myself, to my city, and to everyone who believed in me. I spent my first seven seasons in Cleveland, becoming an All-Star, winning MVPs, and leading the team deep into the playoffs — but that championship still eluded me. In 2010, I made a tough decision and joined the Miami Heat. That move sparked a lot of conversations, but it also sparked results. With Dwyane Wade and Chris Bosh, we built something special. Over four years, we went to four straight NBA Finals and won back-to-back titles in 2012 and 2013. But home was calling. In 2014, I returned to Cleveland with one mission: bring a title to Northeast Ohio. And in 2016, we did the impossible — came back from a 3–1 deficit in the Finals to beat a 73–9 Warriors team. That championship meant everything to me — for my city, for my legacy, for the people who never stopped believing. In 2018, I started a new chapter with the Los Angeles Lakers. Two years later, in the middle of a global pandemic, we won the 2020 NBA Championship inside the Orlando bubble — a title that took everything mentally, physically, and emotionally. Now, after 20+ seasons, I’m still competing at the highest level. I’ve become the NBA’s all-time leading scorer, and I’m still not done. Every game, every moment, I’m writing another page in the story — and I’m grateful for every part of the journey."
